WHY THIS MATTERS

Joseph Weizenbaum was alarmed that people confided in ELIZA even when they knew it was a program. The distance between what the machine does (mechanical word-shuffling, shown above) and what we feel (that something understands us) is the ELIZA effect. It did not end in 1966: it is exactly the gap we navigate with today’s chatbots.
